Keep good hygiene

By following good respiratory hygiene you protect the people around you from viruses that cause colds, flu and COVID-19.

To ensure good hygiene you should:

  • Regularly and thoroughly clean your hands with either an alcohol-based hand rub or soap and water. This eliminates germs that may be on your hands, including viruses.
  • Cover your mouth and nose with your bent elbow or a tissue when you cough or sneeze. Dispose of the used tissue immediately into a closed bin and wash your hands.
  • Cleaning and disinfection: Clean and disinfect frequently-touched objects and surfaces in your home and workplace regularly. Use products that are effective against the COVID-19 virus.
  • Clean and disinfect surfaces frequently, especially those which are regularly touched, such as door handles, faucets and phone screens.
  • Avoid touching your face: Refrain from touching your eyes, nose, and mouth as much as possible, as these areas can serve as entry points for the virus.
  • Stay home when sick: If you have symptoms of COVID-19, such as fever, cough, or difficulty breathing, stay home, and seek medical advice. Follow the guidance of local health authorities regarding testing and self-isolation.
