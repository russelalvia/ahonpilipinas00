How COVID-19 impacted vulnerable communities in the Philippines



When the COVID-19 pandemic began to affect people around the world in mid-2020, the World Bank launched COVID-19 surveys to track the pandemic's social and economic impacts on communities. The community survey conducted in collaboration with the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) in the Philippines provided important insights into how to best deliver pandemic response and recovery programs aimed at supporting the poorest and most vulnerable rural communities. Respondents included community volunteers and barangay (village) officials from some of the country's poorest communities, as identified by the country's current national community-driven development project.

The first round of the community survey had 180 respondents representing 101 barangays in August 2020. The second round, which will take place in April 2021, will include 200 respondents from 135 barangays spread across the three main groups of islands: Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao.
Respondents were asked to share their perspectives on the situation in their communities using a standard questionnaire designed to capture the community's socioeconomic conditions. These findings reflect community leaders' general observations based on their perceptions and knowledge of their respective communities.

Economic impacts on communities

COVID-19 has had a significant impact on rural livelihoods. Loss of income and job opportunities were major issues in the Philippines' poor communities.
Disaster-prone communities struggled more with COVID-19 restrictions and their severe economic impact. According to the findings, the most pressing issues prior to and during COVID-19 were:

• Lack of income opportunities and pay cuts were already issues, but the pandemic had exacerbated them significantly.
• Communities reported that there was still insufficient food supply and health, sanitation, and nutrition issues during the pandemic.

Because the communities that were surveyed are already a subset of the poorest and most vulnerable communities in the Philippines, the economic impact of COVID-19 is especially concerning. Following critical work and pay misfortunes, networks are in danger of additional expansions in destitution.
In August 2020, employment misfortunes were especially serious in the development area (56%) and public transportation (52%) while aggregate employment misfortunes were seen among casual laborers like clothing ladies, beauticians, and laborers in little flasks; and in informal retail settings like markets, street vendors, and "sari-sari" stores. According to reports, 70% of communities in formal agriculture and 61% in small-scale farming saw significant job losses in the farming sector.

By the second round of the survey, which took place in April 2021, the situation had somewhat improved. The area that saw the greatest upgrades was retail, where the revealed rate of employment misfortunes diminished by 13%. However, job cuts continued to have the greatest impact on public transportation drivers and construction workers (56 percent and 52 percent, respectively).

Gender and Women's roles

Women and men were found to be equally affected by job and income loss by half of the respondents, while women were found to be more affected by it by 29%. Due to COVID-19, women were also identified as one of the most in need of assistance.

According to the survey, a lot of people were worried about the safety and effectiveness of vaccines (86 percent).
