What is a COVAX?



COVID19 Vaccine Global AccessCOVAX is a global initiative to ensure fair, equal access for each of the countries that participate in this project. COVAX is a collaboration of the Coalition for Epidemic Preparedness InnovationsCEPI, Gavi, Vaccine Alliance and WHO with UNICEF.

The COVAX solution provides the largest and most diverse portfolio of COVID-19 vaccine candidates, and the Gavi Advance Market Commitment for COVID-19 Vaccines (Gavi Covax AMC) is a binding contract to stimulate suppliers to invest in low-income countries. It supports high-risk populations in LICs and LMICs, as part of the new COVAX Facility.

COVAX is one of the four pillars of the Access to COVID-19 Tools (ACT) Accelerator, an initiative formed by the WHO, the French government, the European Commission, and the Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation. It aims to bring together governments, businesses, civil society, scientists, philanthropists, and global health organizations.

The ACT Accelerator aims to raise funds for accelerated R&D, production, and global equitable access to COVID-19 tests, therapies, and vaccines. The four pillars are as follows:

• Vaccines (COVAX)
• Diagnostics
• Therapeutics
• Health Systems

COVAX's objectives

COVAX initially intended to deliver 2 billion doses of COVID-19 vaccine by the end of 2021, focusing on low-to-middle income countries. This amounts to roughly 20% of the vaccine requirements of the participating countries.

Through this mechanism, over 90 low- and middle-income countries will be eligible to receive 1 billion doses of the COVID-19 vaccine at a low (estimated at 1.60 USD) or no cost. The majority of the G20 countries are represented, including China.
